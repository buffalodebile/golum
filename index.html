How it works

The strategy rotates between a leveraged Nasdaq-100 ETP and leveraged Gold, following simple, fixed rules. Signals are computed daily after the US close and executed automatically the next day, with no discretion.

  • Trend following. When the Nasdaq is in a medium-term uptrend, the portfolio holds leveraged Nasdaq, balanced with gold. When the trend breaks, it rotates fully into gold.
  • Volatility scaling. When market turbulence rises, Nasdaq exposure is automatically scaled down before trouble compounds.
  • Protective stop. A trailing stop on the whole portfolio cuts risk decisively in deep drawdowns.

The exact parameters are the result of years of research and walk-forward validation, and are kept private.

FAQ

Is this real money or just a backtest?
Both, on one chart. Left of the dashed line is a backtest on historical data, a model. Right of it is a real-money account, traded live on eToro and Interactive Brokers, updated daily.
Why doesn't the live curve match the backtest?
It never will, exactly. The model assumes idealized prices and costs; live trading has real instruments, real spreads and fees, execution a day after the signal, and capital that was deployed gradually at the start. Treat the model as the idea and the live curve as the reality.
How risky is it?
Very. It uses leveraged products that can lose value fast, including in choppy, sideways markets (volatility decay). The backtested model has fallen more than 60% from its peak at the worst. You should expect deep drawdowns and only invest money you can afford to lose.
Can I lose more than I put in?
No. The strategy holds leveraged ETPs, not margin or CFDs, so a position can't take you below zero. You can still lose a large part of what you invest.
How often does it trade?
Rarely. It only acts when the trend flips, the stop triggers, or the split drifts too far. Most days it does nothing at all.
