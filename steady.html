How it works

Balanced spreads its money across three building blocks: equities, gold and bonds. A trend filter watches the stock market and quietly reduces equity exposure when it weakens, so the portfolio leans on its calmer holdings in rough patches. The rules are simple and fixed, rebalanced on a set monthly schedule and executed the next day, with no discretion.

Diversification
Equities power the growth, while gold and bonds tend to hold up when stocks fall. Holding all three together smooths the ride and shrinks the deep drawdowns.
Trend filter
When the broad stock market loses its medium-term uptrend, equity exposure is automatically trimmed and shifted toward the defensive holdings, then restored when the trend recovers.
Calm by design
No leverage, a fixed monthly cadence and only a handful of changes a year. The goal is steady compounding with shallow losses, not the highest possible return.

The exact mix and signal settings are the result of research and walk-forward validation, and are kept private.

Important disclaimers

Simulated / paper, not live. Balanced is not traded with real capital. The whole curve is a model: left of the dashed line it is a backtest on historical data with cost assumptions; right of the dashed line it is a daily paper track computed from real market prices. Neither is a real-money track record, and the strategy is not currently available to copy.

Lower risk, not no risk. Balanced uses no leverage and diversifies across equities, gold and bonds, so its worst modelled drawdown was modest, around -12%. Even so, diversified portfolios can fall, and gold and bonds can drop at the same time as stocks.
